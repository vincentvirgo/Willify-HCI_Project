Taylor Swift redefined pop music and culture on her own terms,
seamlessly blending country, pop, and indie-folk through an
authentic and expansive lens. The multi-platinum 11X GRAMMY
Award winner has become one of the most successful and
influential artists of the modern era. 2008's Diamond-certified
Fearless established her as a pop phenomenon, with hits like
"Love Story" and "You Belong With Me" dominating charts
worldwide. Her self-titled debut album in 2006 set the stage for
her meteoric rise.

In 2014, "Shake It Off" from the 7X Platinum 1989 won "Best Pop
Solo Performance" and earned several Grammy nominations.
The album itself won "Album of the Year," making Taylor the first
woman to win the award twice for her own solo albums. In 2020,
Folklore and Evermore showcased her storytelling prowess,
with Folklore winning "Album of the Year" at the Grammys,
making her the first female artist to win the award three times.

Her re-recorded albums, starting with Fearless (Taylor's
Version) in 2021, have been a commercial and critical success,
reclaiming her artistic legacy. In 2022, Red (Taylor's Version)
featured the 10-minute version of "All Too Well," which became
an instant classic and topped the Billboard Hot 100, setting
records for the longest song to ever do so. Taylor's 2019 album
Lover marked her third consecutive #1 debut on the Billboard
200, and "ME!" with Brendon Urie.
